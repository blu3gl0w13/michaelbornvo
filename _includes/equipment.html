Equipment

The right equipment can make or break a good voice-over project. Michael Born Voice-Overs uses the following professional equipment.

  • Studio

    Every voice-over artist knows that using a sound isolation booth is paramount to making a quality recording. A proper setup will not only keep as much sound out as possible but will enhance the sound experience inside the booth itself making sure each frequency is well balanced with the others. Incorrect setup can increase the bass frequency or create too much echo. Michael Born uses a setup that achieves a healthy balance of sound isolation and proper acoustics when recording voice-overs.

  • Microphones

    Michael maintains an inventory of top of the line industry standard microphones such as the Neumann TLM-103, Shure SM7B, and the Electro-Voice RE20. If your project requires a specific microphone, Michael will do his best to accommodate your specific project requirements.

  • Audio Interface

    Michael Born Voice-Overs uses a Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 Audio Interface and a dbx 286s Microphone Pre-Amp for all recordings. Depending on the microphone used, Michael has the ability to configure these devices in order to maintain the best sound for each microphone in his inventory.

  • Digital Audio Workstation

    Michael primarily uses Reaper for making and editing all recordings but has the option and experience to use Logic Pro X if your project requires.

Equipment

The right equipment can make or break a good voice-over project. Michael Born Voice-Overs uses the following professional equipment.

  • Studio

    Michael Born uses a sound isolation booth with reverberation reduction to achieve a healthy balance of sound isolation and proper acoustics when recording voice-overs so that your project is delivered exactly to your artistic specification.

  • Microphones

    Michael maintains an inventory of top of the line industry standard microphones including the Neumann TLM-103, Sennheiser MKH 416, Shure SM7B, and the Electro-Voice RE20. If your project requires a specific microphone, Michael will do his best to accommodate your specific project requirements.

  • Audio Interface

    Michael Born uses a Focusrite Scarlett 2i2 Audio Interface and a dbx 286s Microphone Pre-Amp for all recordings. Depending on the microphone used, Michael has the ability to configure these devices in order to maintain the best sound for each microphone in his inventory.

  • Digital Audio Workstation

    Michael primarily uses Reaper for making and editing all recordings but has the option and experience to use Logic Pro X if your project requires.
